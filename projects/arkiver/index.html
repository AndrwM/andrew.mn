Arkiver

Designing an Application Interface to Elegantly Capture, Curate, and Share Memories

Take Back Control of Your Digital ‘Moments’

The founders of Arkiver first approached me to provide creative direction and user interface mockups to iterate on their existing user interface. The app was simple in concept. Give users storage for all their internet content (‘moments’) scattered across various social media sites. My job was to prototype how one of these moments would operate across all screen sizes—providing a consistent experience with any device.

Full / Desktop View

Featured, a moment in its regular state and edit state along with a prototype of the collapsable, left navigation.

Tablet / Slim View

This is the moment view with its metadata stacked vertically for design for portrait tablets.

Mobile View

The flow for viewing and editing a moment on mobile devices. The tags, people, and category edit states are relagated to an additional screen to account for the necessary larger hitspaces needed on smaller devices.

Other Interface Elements

Throughout our engagement I consulted with Arkiver to prototype more interaction-heavy views for their app. These include login, bulk editing, moment sharing, and user onboarding. This is a selection of a few of the final protypes.

Sorting & Bulk Editing

Filtering, sorting, and bulk editing moments is intended to be a desktop-only feature. These mockups showcase a flow for the filtering and bulk editing moments.

Project Summary

The Arkiver founders were thrilled with my work and since our first engagement we maintain a regular user interface design consulting relationship for prototyping new features and finessing current application interactions.
