A New Mini-Site for an Innovative, New Product Line

When the Marketing team at The Iron Yard came knocking and said they were planning to rebuild the marketing site from the the ground up, I was excited to dig in with them for 6 months and lead the engineering of the design system for the site.

A major pain-point of the previous marketing site was its never-ending technical debt. There was still legacy code from when the company consisted of the 3 original founders. As with any quickly growing company, the needs of the site had been rapidly changing for years. The codebase had been converted to Jekyl, Wordpress, Ruby On Rails, Adobe Experience Manager, and back to Rails again all the while shifting to accommodate new goals. The technical debt had grown to an unmanageable level and it showed: unpredictable behavior, leaky page memory, and inconsistent styles everywhere.

Borrowing from the experience that I had gained building Newline, I had some keen ideas on how to structure the new site. The site's structure used the ITCSS front-end structuring system championed by Mina Markham and Harry Roberts.

Checkout The Live Site

Following launch, the website's bounce rate decreased 28.67% and the average session improved by 18.26%. Conversions of visitor to admissions lead increased by 98%! The leadership was thrilled! This project would't have been successful without the excellent designers and data analysts in the marketing team.
